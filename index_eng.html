Air Quality Index (AQI)

Air Quality Index (AQI) is a key indicator used to assess the level of atmospheric pollution and its potential impact on human health.

This index is based on the average daily concentrations of various substances in the atmospheric air. This approach allows us to take into account the effects of long-term exposure to pollutants on the environment and human health. The Atmospheric Pollution Index is a tool that allows you to summarize and analyze air quality data collected in a certain region over a certain period of time. By assessing the content of various pollutants, such as toxic gases, particulate matter, and others, the ISA helps determine the level of risk to public health and take appropriate measures to improve air quality and protect public health.

Questions and Answers

In this section you can get answers to your questions.

WHO data indicate that air pollution is one of the leading causes of premature death, exacerbating diseases such as stroke, lung cancer, cardiovascular, and respiratory conditions, among others.

According to the UNEP, air pollution is caused mainly by five human activities: transport, industry, waste, agriculture and household.

To combat air pollution, it is necessary to develop public transport, use electric vehicles, use the best available industrial technologies and renewable energy sources. It is important to reduce the use of fossil fuels, actively green cities, raise public awareness and implement strict environmental laws.

In 2024, the most polluted country in terms of atmospheric air quality (PM2.5) is Bangladesh. It is followed by Pakistan and India.

According to the IQAir rating, Kazakhstan ranked 40th among 134 countries in terms of air quality.
